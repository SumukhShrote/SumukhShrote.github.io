About

I am an M.S.E. Robotics student at the University of Pennsylvania, working at the intersection of physical AI, embodied agents, and robot learning. My research centers on enabling robots to acquire generalizable skills through interaction — building systems that can perceive, reason about, and act within complex environments. I am interested in the long arc: robots that model the world and use that understanding to behave robustly across diverse, unstructured settings.

Research

Physical AI

Intelligence that lives in and acts on the physical world — not in a simulator, not in a dataset. I am interested in how robots can acquire the physical intuition needed to operate robustly across real environments without brittle, hand-coded assumptions.

Embodied AI

Closed-loop agents that perceive, plan, and act in real time. My work explores how grounding language and reasoning in sensorimotor experience leads to more capable and generalizable robotic behavior.

World Models

Learned internal representations of environment dynamics that allow robots to plan, adapt, and generalize. I view world models as the missing layer between perception and action in current robotic systems.

Robot Learning

Reinforcement learning, imitation learning, and sim-to-real transfer applied to physically demanding tasks. I focus on sample-efficient methods that close the gap between simulation and deployment on real hardware.

Work

  • Aerial Catching with RL — Crazyflie Manipulator Isaac Lab

    End-to-end sim-to-real pipeline for ball-catching on a brushless Crazyflie aerial manipulator. Includes system identification, domain randomization, and real hardware deployment with a 3D ball-tracking perception system.

  • Quadcopter Racing with Curriculum RL Isaac Lab · Isaac Sim

    Two-phase curriculum — standard gate racing then powerloop maneuver — within a single training run. Custom phantom-gate representation and hybrid reward function including maneuver-specific shaping terms.

  • Semantic Perception & Scene Graph — Boston Dynamics Spot ROS 2 · YOLO-World · LiDAR

    Real-time open-vocabulary object detection fused with Ouster LiDAR for 3D landmark localization on Spot. First stage of a broader semantic scene graph pipeline for robot-centric world modeling.

    Custom Model Predictive Path Integral controller that evaluates 150 trajectory rollouts using real-time elevation and traversability maps. Sub-30 cm goal accuracy; deployed on physical hardware.
