About

I am an M.S.E. Robotics student at the University of Pennsylvania, working at the intersection of physical AI, embodied agents, and robot learning. My research centers on enabling robots to acquire generalizable skills through interaction — building systems that can perceive, reason about, and act within complex environments. I am interested in the long arc: robots that model the world and use that understanding to behave robustly across diverse, unstructured settings.

Research

Physical AI

Intelligence that lives in and acts on the physical world. I am interested in how robots can acquire the physical intuition needed to operate robustly across real environments without brittle, hand-coded assumptions.

Embodied AI

Closed-loop agents that perceive, plan, and act in real time. My work explores how grounding language and reasoning in sensorimotor experience leads to more capable and generalizable robotic behavior.

World Models

Learned internal representations of environment dynamics that allow robots to plan, adapt, and generalize. I view world models as the missing layer between perception and action in current robotic systems.

Robot Learning

Reinforcement learning, imitation learning, and sim-to-real transfer applied to physically demanding tasks. I focus on sample-efficient methods that close the gap between simulation and real hardware deployment.

Foundation Models for Robotics

Large pretrained models adapted for robotic perception, planning, and control. I am interested in how vision-language models and generalist policies can be grounded in physical tasks without losing broad capabilities.

Generalist Robotics

Building robotic systems that transfer across tasks, embodiments, and environments. The goal is not a robot that solves one problem well, but one that solves a wide class of problems with minimal retraining.
